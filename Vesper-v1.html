05— A letter from the founders
MARISOL A.CO‑FOUNDER
JUN‑HO L.CO‑FOUNDER · PHD

We started Vesper because we were tired — quietly, for years — of the bottles on our own counters.

Between the two of us we counted thirty‑one supplements in our kitchens. Most did nothing we could measure. Several made us feel worse. All of them had been sold to us as essential.

So we spent four years, in laboratories in Singapore and on farms in Oaxaca, reducing the answer. Twelve molecules. Two capsules. One honest gesture. Anything that did not earn its place was left out.

This is the bottle we wished someone had made for us. Thank you for the morning, and for the trust.

Marisol & Jun‑ho

13— Before you begin

Curiosities,
answered in kind.

/ 01When will I feel something?
Most of our readers describe a quiet shift around week three — steadier mornings, softer afternoons. Bloodwork moves before the body does; if you test before beginning and again at day 90, the numbers will tell you what the mornings already have.
/ 02Can I take Vesper with other supplements?
Vesper is designed as a complete daily foundation — most of our readers stop taking the rest. If you are on prescription medication, or taking high‑dose single vitamins, please forward the list to our medical desk before you begin. They answer within a working day.
/ 03Why only two capsules?
Because twelve was vanity, and four was overkill. Two is what the dosages honestly require, and two is what we could ask of your morning without becoming a chore. Everything about Vesper is sized for the life you already lead.
/ 04Where do you source from?
Lichen from Finland for the D₃. Pacific kelp for iodine, harvested off the coast of Hokkaido. Natto‑fermented K₂ from a small family kitchen in Kyoto. A full, audited map of every ingredient ships with your first bottle, folded inside the lid.
/ 05Is the bottle really glass?
Yes — hand‑finished amber glass, weighted to sit quietly on a counter, engraved with the formula number and your first name if you would like. Refill pouches ship monthly, each one compostable, in a smaller cardboard envelope that fits any mailbox.
