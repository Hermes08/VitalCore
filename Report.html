There is a version of this letter we could have written that would be shorter and more triumphant. We are profitable for the third year in a row, in a category where most of our peers are not. Revenue grew 38%. We hired six people without losing anyone. Nobody owes us money we cannot collect.

That version would be dishonest. The truth is that FY26 was the first year we felt the gravity of being the brand people write about when they want to make a point about the wellness industry. It flattered us, then it tired us. We turned down two acquisition approaches, three accelerator partnerships, and roughly forty podcast interviews. We said no to Amazon for the fourth time. We also made a formulation error in batch V26-030 that cost us €211,000 to recall and, more importantly, the trust of 1,904 customers. You'll read about that on page 22, in the same size type as the good news.

What we are learning, slowly, is that the hardest part of building a calm company is not the refusing — it is the staying calm once the refusing has worked. Growth is the stimulant we are all addicted to, and having said no to venture capital does not make us immune to wanting a bigger number next year. We spent a lot of Q3 arguing about whether to launch a men's line, a children's line, and a bundled gift set for the holidays. We launched none of them. That may turn out to be correct and it may turn out to be a mistake. We will tell you next April.

The thing we are most proud of this year is not in the P&L. It is that every batch we shipped has a published certificate of analysis, every formula we sell names its suppliers, and every person who wrote to our team got a reply from a human inside four working hours. Those three sentences are the entire brand. If we keep them true for another ten years, the numbers will take care of themselves. If we let any one of them slip to save a quarter, the numbers will not save us.

Thank you for reading. Thank you, especially, for noticing the things we are trying to do quietly. We remain, as always, available at a real email address.

L. MoreauCo-founder · formulation
T. LindqvistCo-founder · operations

The V26-030 recall · what happened

Full post-mortem · published 14 Oct 2025
Aug 11 T=0
Routine third-party retest flags inconsistency Batch V26-030 of our Ashwagandha KSM-66 came back with withanolide concentration 11% lower than spec. Our retained sample agreed. No safety issue; an efficacy issue. Eurofins · Copenhagen · #LAB-41291
Aug 12 +1 d
Decision to recall, not to wait We could have quietly downgraded the spec. We did not. Recall initiated by the founders at 09:40 CET before any regulator asked. Internal memo · public 14 Oct
Aug 13 +2 d
1,904 customers notified directly Personal email from Léa. Refund or replacement from next batch, customer's choice. 89% chose replacement. 11% chose refund; we reimbursed shipping both ways. 100% replied within 9 days
Aug 28 +17 d
Root cause identified Supplier's harvest from a new farm in Madhya Pradesh. Standardisation process was correct; input material was weaker. We changed nothing about testing — testing worked. We changed the supplier's obligation to notify us of any harvest-source changes. Total direct cost · €211,000
Oct 14 +64 d
Public post-mortem published Full technical write-up on vesper.house/recall including lab data, every email sent to customers, and the supplier contract change. 68,000 reads in week one. 340 new subscribers cited it as the reason they signed up. Most-shared page FY26

What we intend.

Six commitments. We will grade ourselves against them in next year's report, on this same page, in this same size type.

01Reformulate Ashwagandha with a different standardised extract and publish full side-by-side comparison data.Q2
02Publish every supplier name and farm location for all 26 SKUs, not just our top five.Q2
03Decide in public whether to open a US warehouse. Publish carbon, cost, and customer-experience math either way.Q2
04Run the first Vesper-funded clinical trial (double-blind, placebo) on Daily N° 01, with pre-registered endpoints. We will publish even if the result is unflattering.Q3
